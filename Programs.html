Mental Health Counselling

Description of Services: The Women Heal Foundation offers professional mental health counselling services designed to support women through a variety of life challenges, including anxiety, depression, trauma, relationship issues, and more. Our licensed therapists provide individual and group counselling sessions that are tailored to meet the unique needs of each woman. We also offer specialized counselling for women who have experienced domestic violence, abuse, or other forms of trauma.

Eligibility: Our mental health counselling services are available to all women aged 18 and older. We particularly encourage those who are struggling with mental health issues, survivors of abuse, and women facing significant life transitions to access our services.

How to Access: Women interested in our counselling services can schedule an appointment by visiting our website and filling out the intake form. Once submitted, a member of our team will contact you to arrange an initial consultation. Counselling sessions can be conducted online through our secure telehealth platform.

Educational Workshops

Topics covered: educational workshops are designed to empower women with knowledge and skills that can help them navigate life’s challenges. Topics include mental health awareness, stress management, self-care practices, financial literacy, career development, and healthy relationships. We also offer specialized workshops focused on parenting, leadership skills, and personal growth.

Schedule:Workshops are held monthly, with some sessions offered as one-time events and others as part of a series. Each workshop is led by experienced professionals and subject matter experts, ensuring that participants receive high-quality, practical information.

Registration Information: To participate in our workshops, women can register online through our website. We offer both free and paid workshops, with scholarships available for those in financial need. Once registered, participants will receive a confirmation email with details on the workshop location or online access instructions.

Support Groups

Description of Support Groups: The Women Heal Foundation hosts a variety of support groups to provide women with a safe space to share their experiences and connect with others who are facing similar challenges. Our support groups include:

  • Domestic Violence: A group for women who have experienced domestic violence, offering peer support and guidance on healing and moving forward.
  • Mental Health Peer Support: A group for women dealing with anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues, focused on mutual support and coping strategies.
  • Single Mothers: A group dedicated to single mothers, providing a supportive community to discuss the unique challenges they face and share resources.
    • Grief and Loss: A group for women coping with the loss of a loved one, offering a compassionate space to express emotions and find comfort.

    How to Join: Women interested in joining a support group can sign up through our website. Each group meets weekly, and participants are encouraged to attend regularly to build connections and gain the full benefit of the group experience. Our support groups are free to join and are facilitated by trained professionals who ensure a safe and supportive environment.

    Body Positivity & Self-Esteem Building

    Description of Services: Our Body Positivity & Self-Esteem Building program is dedicated to helping women embrace their bodies and develop a strong sense of self-worth, regardless of their shape, size, or past experiences. This program includes workshops, support groups, and one-on-one coaching designed to challenge negative body image, promote self-love, and empower women to feel confident in their own skin. We address issues such as body shaming, societal pressures, and the impact of trauma on self-esteem, providing tools and strategies for women to rebuild their confidence.

    Eligibility: This program is open to all women who struggle with body image issues, low self-esteem, or have experienced trauma that has affected their perception of themselves. We especially encourage women who feel isolated or judged because of their appearance to participate.

    How to Access: Women can join this program by signing up through our website. The program includes a mix of group workshops, online resources, and individual coaching sessions. Participants will have access to a supportive community where they can share their experiences and receive guidance from body positivity advocates and mental health professionals. Regular sessions are held both in person and online, ensuring that all women, regardless of location, can benefit from the program.

    Advocacy

    Information on Advocacy Efforts: The Women Heal Foundation is committed to advocating for women’s rights and mental health awareness on a local, national, and global scale. Our advocacy efforts include:

  • Public Awareness Campaigns: We run campaigns to raise awareness about mental health issues, domestic violence, and the importance of women's empowerment. These campaigns include social media initiatives, community events, and partnerships with other organizations.
  • Policy Advocacy: We work with policymakers to influence legislation that supports women’s mental health, protection from violence, and equal opportunities. Our advocacy team engages in research, drafts policy recommendations, and participates in lobbying efforts.
  • Community Outreach: We actively engage with communities to provide education on women’s rights and mental health, distributing informational materials and hosting outreach events to empower women at the grassroots level.

    • How to Get Involved: Individuals can get involved in our advocacy efforts by joining our advocacy network, volunteering for campaign activities, or participating in policy advocacy initiatives. We also welcome donations to support our advocacy work. To join our network or learn more about upcoming advocacy opportunities, visit our website and sign up for our advocacy newsletter.
